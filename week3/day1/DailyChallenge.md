Daily challenge: Real-world Data Analysis Scenarios
What you will learn

    Understanding the practical applications of data analysis in real-world scenarios.
    Insight into how data analysis drives decision-making and problem-solving.
    Knowledge of various data analysis methodologies and their impacts.


What you will create

    A detailed analysis of the role and impact of data analysis in the selected story.
    A research-based presentation on a chosen real-life case where data analysis played a key role.


Instructions

Task: Find and present in writing a recent news story or business case study where data analysis played a crucial role. This could involve situations where data analysis led to a breakthrough, influenced a major decision, or uncovered significant trends impacting the business or societal context.

    Step 1: Search for a relevant case or news story. Consider sources like business journals, news websites, or scientific publications.


    Step 2: Analyze the role of data analysis in the chosen story. Pay attention to what data was analyzed, the methods used, and the outcomes or decisions driven by the analysis.

    Step 3: Ask yourself how the data analysis impacted the situation. Consider questions like: What would have been different without data analysis? How did data analysis contribute to solving a problem or making a decision?
    Step 4: Present your findings in writing, Focus on explaining the significance of data analysis in the context of your chosen story.

The aim of this activity is to provide practical insights into how data analysis is applied in real-world scenarios and to appreciate its value in shaping modern decision-making processes.

My Presentation:
One recent story that describes how data analysis played a crucial role in a business decision is "Feeling The Churn: Why Netflix Cancels Shows After A Couple Of Seasons & Why They Can’t Move To New Homes" (by Nellie Andreeva, 18.3.2019, https://deadline.com/2019/03/netflix-tv-series-cancellations-strategy-one-day-at-a-time-1202576297/). The story discusses how Netflix's policy of cancelling many series after 3 seasons is influenced by its use of data.
The data Netflix analyzed in this context are mainly its data on its subscribers: new subscribers and churn (subscribers leaving) over time. We can assume that the methods it used for collecting this data involved looking at its own internal use daata. It found that introducing new series attracts new subscribers, whereas adding additional seasons beyond the third has a lower effect in doing so, and fans dissapointed by series cancellation would not leave the service as long as new series are being released. It found that three seasons are sufficient to satisfy viewers discovering a show, whereas adding more episodes beyond that provides limited added satisfcation. This analysis led to Netflix deciding to cancel many  series after three seasons.
Without data analysis, Netflix would not have had access to these insights, and would likely have continued to run popular series, without knowing that this provided it limited added value. In this case it was data analysis that contributed to the decision to implement this policy. We can expect that this decision led to greater profits for Netflix. Additional analysis could examine the degree this was true, by looking at how profits changed since the implementation of this decision.
